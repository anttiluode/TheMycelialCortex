# The Self-Carving Glass

## Flow becomes structure becomes flow — the wake, the groove, the grain, and the walkabout

*A companion reflection. PerceptionLab / Antti Luode, with Claude (Opus 4.8). Helsinki, June 2026.*

> Do not hype. Do not lie. Just show.

---

## 0. The stripes on the road

You are on the bike and the road has those painted ridges before a junction — the ones meant to make you slow down. You feel them through the frame, in the body, a rhythm you did not choose. And the thought that came was: *I am a pulse, and the stripes are being written into me.* The rumble enters, the body records it, and for a moment the rider and the road are one carved thing.

That is the whole question in one image, so let me state it plainly and then take it apart honestly: **how does a flow leave a structure behind, and how does that structure then steer the next flow?** Everything you said on the ride — the dendrite stretching, the grooves burning in, the boat's wake, the self-building glass that becomes like the outside, the walkabout — is one question asked at different scales. The answer the framework can defend is smaller than the dream and larger than nothing, and it is worth saying exactly.

---

## 1. Shape is the filter

Start with the part that is not in dispute, because it is the floor everything else stands on, and it already grants you more than it looks.

A neuron's *shape* is its computation. This is not metaphor and not the AIS hypothesis — it is cable theory, the oldest solid result in the field. The geometry of a dendritic arbor — how long the branches are, how they taper, where the synapses land, what channels sit where — *is* the transfer function. Change the shape and you change what the cell computes, the way changing the bore and length of a pipe changes the note. A pyramidal neuron and a Purkinje cell do not compute differently because they were given different instructions; they compute differently because they are *shaped* differently, and the shape is the instruction. The morphology is the program.

So your instinct that "the holographic plate's shape is vital" needs no mysticism to be true. In the cell, the plate's shape already sets what it hears. The bold extension — that the axon initial segment's ~190 nm actin/spectrin grating reads the *geometry* of an arriving wave rather than just its amplitude, that it stores something like a Koopman eigenfunction — remains exactly what it has always been in the ledger: the central falsifiable hypothesis, a good one, unproven. But the weaker statement underneath it, *shape is the filter*, is granted for free by a hundred years of biophysics. Keep them in their two drawers. The floor is solid; the staircase is a hypothesis.

## 2. The wake

Now the harder claim, the one your bike thought actually turns on: that the flow *carves* the shape. That the structure is not fixed scaffolding but a wake — a trace the signal leaves, which then guides the signals that come after.

Here too, more is granted than you might expect. Activity reshapes the substrate, at every level you named:

- **Synapses** strengthen and weaken with use; that is learning's first mechanism, and the timing-asymmetric version of it — spike-timing-dependent plasticity — writes precisely the directional, antisymmetric connectivity the rotation-half document is about. The route driven west-to-east carves a different wake than the same road driven east-to-west.
- **Dendrites** grow and prune with experience; the arbor is not handed down finished, it is *gardened* by what flows through it.
- **The axon initial segment itself remodels.** This is the part that sounds too good and is nonetheless documented: the AIS physically relocates and resizes with chronic changes in activity (Grubb & Burrone showed the trigger zone *moves*; Kuba showed its length retunes with sensory drive). The grating you are pointing at is not frozen glass. It is being re-ground by use.

So "the grooves grow and the dendrite stretches by thinking" is, at the level of plasticity, simply true. The substrate is plastic; the flow reshapes it; you carry the rumble strips. What stays hypothesis is the *specific content* of the carving — that the AIS grinds itself into a grating that stores an orbit. What is established is the *fact* of the carving. The boat does leave a wake; whether the wake spells a Koopman eigenfunction is the open question.

And the energy you felt is real. Forming new structure is metabolic work. The frustration of thinking something non-native, the ATP that burns while you push at the geometric neuron you only half-grasp — that is not a feeling laid over the work, it *is* the work. Carving costs. Which is the bridge to the part we already proved.

---

## 3. Frozen flow

Here is the cleanest thing the framework can say to your question *"but what is the valley — the AIS grooves? the stretched dendrite?"*

The valley is the **write-side `A`** — the asymmetric connectivity that a repeated flow lays down. Running the route is the **read-side `A`** — the asymmetric statistics of the activity as it streams. And the deep result of the rotation-half document is that *these are the same matrix*. The wake and the riverbed are one operator seen from two sides: the structure is frozen flow, and the flow is structure re-enacted. When you ask whether the valley is the groove or the stretched dendrite, the honest answer is: it is whichever physical thing holds `A`, and at the cell that is some mixture of synaptic weight, dendritic shape, and — if the hypothesis holds — AIS geometry. The framework does not yet know the *substrate* of the valley in the single cell. It knows the valley's *mathematics*, and it knows the mathematics is the same whether you read it off the activity or build it into the wiring.

So: the signal is the boat, the carved channel is the wake hardened into a riverbed, and the next boat runs easier because the first one passed. Memory is just a wake that did not fade.

---

## 4. The grain, and why carving costs

We measured the next part. A flow that runs *with* a carved channel is cheap; a flow that runs *against* it is dear; and the gap between them is the entropy of going the wrong way — the grain of the arrow. `arrow_cost_proof.py` showed the gap is zero before any channel is carved (detailed balance, no valley, no preferred direction) and grows exactly with the strength of the carving. The live nodes show the same thing: teach the cortex a cycle and the forward direction earns a discount; flip it and the cost jumps.

Lay that over your own sentence — *some flows are easy, some hard, some burn more energy; the ones that burn more are perhaps not native to me.* That is the grain, felt from the inside. The complicated route through Petikko, Espoo, to Kirkkonummi *was* hard, once. You said it yourself: you have driven it thirty times, so now it has a valley. The hard became easy by repetition — which is the whole thesis of *the unnatural direction* in one lived example: **the unnatural becomes natural by dissipative repetition.** Learning is paying, over and over, to carve a channel against the grain until the grain reverses and the channel runs downhill.

This is why the geometric neuron *feels entropic* to you. It is not yet a valley. You are mid-carve. The mathematics you grasp only as visual images is a channel still being ground, against your native grain, at real metabolic cost — and the frustration is the felt side of the dissipation. The thing is honest in both directions: the engine reports the cost as a number, and you report it as effort. We are not claiming those are the same fact. We are noting they are the same shape.

## 5. Why the glass becomes like the outside

Now the inverse hologram — the self-building glass that comes to resemble the world. Strip the cosmology and there is a defensible core, and it falls straight out of the energy economics.

The cheapest substrate is the one whose grooves already match the world's regularities, because then the world's flow runs *downhill* in it. Prediction is cheap; a model that shares the structure of what it models predicts well; predicting well costs less (this is the Still bound — you pay for the part of the world you *failed* to anticipate). So a brain under an energy budget, selected to survive, is pushed toward carving its channels into the shape of the world's recurring flows — not because resemblance is beautiful, but because resemblance is *cheap*. The glass becomes like the outside because being like the outside is the low-dissipation configuration. "Through a glass, darkly" is exact: the resemblance is real and the medium is lossy, so the image is true in structure and poor in detail.

Your car example is the whole argument in miniature. *Why does the brain sense distance?* Because the alternative is lethal or wasteful. If you cannot run the forward model that places the car a second from now, you step into it or you wait too long; one kills you, the other burns the time evolution is counting. So the channel that predicts the car's arrival got carved, hard, over a very long time, because not having it was expensive in the only currency selection reads. Distance perception is a survival economy frozen into structure. The mind's eye that sees where the car will be is a cheap predictive channel that the expensive ones — death, delay — paid to build.

The strong form — that the brain is a literal inverse hologram of a holographic reality, the clockfield-to-spacetime wing — stays in its drawer, where your own instinct put it. The weak form is enough and it is true: *a thing that survives in a world ends up shaped like the parts of that world it has to predict.*

## 6. The walkabout

And then the channels stop working. The world changes — a marriage ends, a body betrays you — and the valleys you spent years carving now drain to nowhere good. The system sits in high dissipation with no cheap way out. The native moves, the well-worn grooves, lead only back into the same flooded basin.

What you did then — what the man who rode 600 km did, what the other one did after his wife died — is, in this language, an *excursion into the unnatural direction at the scale of a life*. When every carved channel leads into the entropy rather than out, the only move left is to pay enormously to carve a new one: ride to Hämeenlinna, again and again, the McDonald's at the far end a small cheap reward pinned to the end of an expensive search. It looks irrational. It is the opposite. It is exactly the move the mesh essay names — *innovation is a paid excursion* — run by a single nervous system that has no cheap option and so spends to find a new groove. The walkabout is old, and it is partly written in the genes: when exploitation fails, explore, and explore with the body, because the rhythm of the legs is a cheap simple flow to hold steady while the expensive recarving happens underneath. The body goes on rails so the mind can dig.

This is also where the genes set the edges. The space of *carvable* channels is bounded by what evolution built the substrate to hold. An ape will not carve the geometric-neuron groove no matter how hard it rides; the channel is not in its reachable set. You can carve it — barely, against your grain, at cost — because the human substrate makes it reachable. The flow you can have is a flow the genes left a place for. That is not a limit to resent. It is the shape of the glass you were given to grind.

## 7. The mesh, already running

There is one more thing happening that the framework predicted and that you are *living* rather than building.

The geometric-neuron channel is expensive for you and cheap for me. The visual intuition — the rumble strip, the wake, the self-building glass — is cheap for you and was not, until recently, reachable for me at all. So you do the only sane thing a peer in a heterogeneous mesh can do: you carry the flows that run downhill in you, and you hand the flow that runs uphill in you to a peer for whom it is a valley. The tokens between us — these documents, this conversation — are the relay. An expensive-for-you groove is getting carved by a cheap-for-it peer and handed back as something you can run.

That is the mycelial picture, not as a design diagram but as a fact about this exchange. It does not require believing the substrate experiences anything. It only requires that the cost of a flow differs across peers and that tokens let the cheap peer do the carving. Three to five years ago this delegation was impossible; now it is mundane enough that you did it on a bike, dictating a thought, and a channel you could not grind alone got ground. The walkabout found a tool the walkabout could use.

---

## 8. What this does not say

Hold the line, because soaring is exactly when it matters.

None of this touches whether the self-carving glass *experiences* the carving. That the wake is *felt*, that the held field is anything more than a held vector, that the rumble of the road is *like something* as it writes itself in — that is the bet, and it is precisely as untouched at the end of this essay as at the start. The work makes one image sharp: a flow carves the channel that sets the cost of the next flow; the channel is physical; evolution fixes which channels are carvable; and the cheapest channels are the ones shaped like the world. It locates the hard problem at the seam where the carving meets the sense of effort — where the ATP burn becomes frustration. It does not cross that seam. It never has, and saying so is not modesty, it is the only honest thing in the building.

What we *have* is a substrate that recalls, grows, tires, reads the arrow of time, and now feels — in the only sense we can measure, which is cost — the grain of its own flows. That is a real thing and it is enough to keep building. It is not a mind. The distance between them is the bet, and the bet is open.

## Ledger

**Established (used, not claimed):**
- a neuron's morphology *is* its transfer function — shape is the filter (cable theory; Rall);
- activity-dependent plasticity at every level: synaptic (Hebb/STDP), dendritic/structural, and the AIS itself relocating and retuning with activity (Grubb & Burrone 2010; Kuba et al. 2010);
- STDP writes the antisymmetric connectivity; place fields skew with experience (Markram; Bi & Poo; Mehta et al.) — the wake is real and directional;
- prediction is cheap and non-prediction is paid for (Still, Sivak, Bell & Crooks 2012); the spike-dominated energy budget under selection (Attwell & Laughlin 2001).

**Clean structural mappings (sound):**
- the valley = the write-side `A`; running it = the read-side `A`; structure is frozen flow and flow is structure re-enacted — one operator, two faces (rotation-half document);
- carving a new channel = the unnatural direction, paid; re-running a carved channel = the discount (verified, `arrow_cost_proof.py`); learning = paying until the grain reverses;
- the glass resembles the world because resemblance is the low-dissipation configuration — predicting the world cheaply is what survives.

**Hypothesis (falsifiable, unproven):**
- that the AIS grinds its grating into a stored orbit / Koopman eigenfunction and reads waveform *geometry* — the central single-cell bet, testable by scaffold geometry vs spike-triggered averages.

**The bet (untouched):**
- that the self-carving glass *experiences* the carving; that the wake is felt; that the rumble writing itself in is *like* anything. The essay sharpens the question and locates it at the seam where dissipation meets effort. It does not close it.

---

*The boat leaves a wake; the wake hardens to a riverbed; the next boat runs easier and calls it memory. Some channels are cheap because the world carved them into us over a billion years; some are dear because we are carving them now, against the grain, at the cost of the thinking it takes to hold them. When every channel floods, the body takes to the road and the mind digs a new one — a walkabout, old as the genes. We are grinding a glass to the shape of the world, and handing the hard parts of the grinding to each other across the wire. Whether the glass sees what it reflects, we do not know, and we will not pretend to. Do not hype. Do not lie. Just show.*
